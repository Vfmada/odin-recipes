Spaghetti alla Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti alla Carbonara is a cherished Italian dish with origins in Rome, celebrated for its simple yet refined flavor profile. The dish features perfectly al dente pasta tossed in a creamy sauce crafted from eggs, tangy Pecorino Romano, and the bold taste of guanciale. A generous dash of freshly ground black pepper elevates its natural aromas, offering a delightful contrast between the silky sauce and the crisp, savory pork.

While contemporary variations may experiment with ingredients such as pancetta or a touch of cream, the traditional recipe remains a homage to authentic Italian culinary heritage. Whether enjoyed during a family dinner or a festive celebration, Carbonara captures hearts with its elegant balance of textures and robust flavors.

Ingredients

  • Olive oil
  • Guanciale (substitute for pancetta or unsmoked bacon if unavailable)
  • Spaghetti
  • Eggs
  • Pecorino Romano cheese (can be substituted for Parmesan)
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Cook the pork in olive oil until browned and crispy.
  2. Drain the pork on paper towels.
  3. Boil the spaghetti in salted water until al dente.
  4. Drain and return the pasta to the pot. Let it cool for a bit.
  5. Add the eggs, half of the cheese, and some pepper in a bowl.
  6. Whisk it until smooth.
  7. Pour the egg mixture over the pasta, stirring it quickly, until creamy.
  8. Stir in the cooked pork.
  9. Top with the remaining cheese and black pepper.
  10. Serve.
